How to Spot & Stop Phishing AI Awareness
  • Double-check the sender: Don’t trust email display names alone. Always inspect the email address.
  • Check links: Hover over links to see the real web address. Be extra cautious with misspellings or unusual domains.
  • Be alert for AI-generated messages: Attackers use AI to craft convincing emails, fake login pages, and even deepfake calls.
  • Never share sensitive info: Real companies or IT teams will never ask for your password over email or chat.
  • Look for urgency: Messages that demand immediate action (“Your account will be locked!”) are classic red flags.
  • Check for odd language: Even AI-written text can have strange phrasing or be overly generic.
What To Do If You Suspect Phishing
  • Report suspicious emails or sites to your IT/Security team right away.
  • Do NOT enter passwords or download attachments if you have doubts.
  • If you clicked a suspicious link, change your password immediately and notify IT.
Stay safe—question unexpected messages, and always verify requests before acting.
